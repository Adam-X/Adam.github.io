A DUAL-PATH EFFICIENT EEG ENCODER FOR BRAIN-ASSISTED TARGET SPEAKER EXTRACTION


Wang Xiang, Xue Zhang, Cunhang Fan, Jun Xue, Juan Liu, Ming Li

School of Computer Science, Wuhan University, Wuhan, China


Abstract

Brain-assisted target speaker extraction can selectively extract the target speaker’s speech by decoding the listener’s brain activity from electroencephalogram (EEG) signals. However, EEG signals have low signal-to-noise ratio, high dimensionality, and non-stationary characteristics, making it very challenging to efficiently extract advanced features. To address these limitations, this paper proposes a dual-path Receptance Weighted Key Value-like (RWKV) EEG encoder network designed to mitigate the inefficiency and difficulty of modeling long sequences and weak correlations inherent in EEG signals. Specifically, the proposed dual-path RWKV EEG encoder overcomes the limitations of traditional EEG encoders by applying space and channel interaction modules to EEG data, enabling the model to simultaneously capture multidi-mensional spatiotemporal frequency information in EEG signals. This dual-path collaborative design enables the model to mimic the information processing mechanism of the brain and integrate global dynamics while deepening local feature extraction, these operations are conducted with linear computational complexity. The experimental results on three publicly available datasets show that the proposed model significantly outperforms current state-of-the-art methods in different evaluation metrics.
